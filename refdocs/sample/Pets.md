# Pets

## Luna (Dog)

**Breed:** Golden Retriever / Lab mix
**Age:** 5 years
**Weight:** ~60 lbs
**Color:** Golden with a white chest patch

### Feeding
- **Twice a day**: 7:00am and 5:30pm
- 1.5 cups of dry food per meal (the big bag of Blue Buffalo in the pantry, bottom shelf)
- Her bowl is the stainless steel one by the back door
- She gets a small scoop of pumpkin puree mixed in with dinner (the canned stuff in the fridge, NOT pie filling). It helps her digestion.
- Fresh water always available — she drinks a lot, refill her water bowl at least twice a day
- **No table scraps.** She will stare at you with enormous sad eyes. She is lying. She has already eaten.

### Medications
- **Monthly heartworm/flea chew**: given on the 1st of each month. The box is in the pantry next to her food. She thinks it's a treat, so this is easy — just hand it to her.
- No other current medications

### Walking
- **Morning walk**: 20-30 min, ideally before 8am. She has a lot of energy in the morning.
- **Evening walk**: 15-20 min after dinner. Can be shorter if it's raining, she doesn't love rain.
- **Leash**: the red one on the hook by the front door. She pulls a little at first but settles down.
- **Route**: we usually go left on Ash, up to Sunnyside Park, loop around, come back. She knows the route.
- She's friendly with other dogs but gets overexcited. If she starts pulling toward another dog, have her sit first. She knows "sit" and "wait."
- **She barks at skateboards and scooters.** Just redirect her attention and keep walking. It's not aggression, she just doesn't understand wheels.
- Poop bags are in the leash pouch and in the drawer by the front door.

### Yard
- The backyard is fenced (she can't get out) so she can be let out anytime. The back door is the sliding glass door off the kitchen.
- She likes to dig near the garden beds. We're fighting a losing battle on this.
- **Don't leave her outside alone for more than 30 min** — she'll bark at squirrels and the neighbors will hear about it

### Behavior Notes
- She sleeps in our bedroom on her dog bed (the big green one). If we're not home, she might sleep by the front door waiting. That's normal and a little sad.
- She knows: sit, stay, down, come, wait, leave it, go to your bed
- She's good with kids — very gentle with Noa
- She gets nervous during thunderstorms. If there's a storm, let her come sit with you. She'll tremble but she just wants to be near someone.
- **She is NOT allowed on the couch.** She will try. She knows she's not supposed to. Don't fall for it.

### Vet
- Hawthorne Animal Hospital, Dr. Nguyen: (503) 555-8200
- She's up to date on all vaccinations
- If something seems wrong: she'll usually stop eating first, that's the tell

## Mochi (Cat)

**Breed:** Domestic shorthair (tuxedo pattern)
**Age:** 3 years
**Weight:** ~10 lbs

### Feeding
- **Twice a day**: morning and evening, roughly when Luna eats
- 1/4 cup dry food (the small bag of Purina on the second shelf in the pantry) + one small can of wet food (the Fancy Feast variety pack, also pantry)
- Her food and water are on the counter in the laundry room — up high so Luna can't eat it
- She's a grazer, she won't finish her wet food right away. That's fine, just toss it if it's still there after a few hours.

### Litter Box
- In the laundry room, behind the door
- Scoop daily (the scoop is right next to it). We use the clumping litter in the bucket under the utility sink.
- Full litter change once a week (David usually does Sunday). Dump it in a trash bag, refill about 3 inches deep.

### Behavior
- She's indoor-only. **Do not let her outside.** She'll try to dart out when you open the front door. Block her with your foot.
- She's shy with strangers — she'll hide for the first day or two, probably under our bed or in the closet. That's normal. Leave food and water out, she'll eat when you're not looking.
- Once she warms up, she's very affectionate. She'll sit on your lap while you watch TV.
- She and Luna coexist fine. Mochi will swat Luna's nose if Luna gets too close. Luna respects this arrangement.
- She likes the window perch in the living room (the shelf with the blanket on it). That's her spot.

### Vet
- Same as Luna: Hawthorne Animal Hospital, Dr. Nguyen
- She's spayed and up to date on shots
- She's due for a dental cleaning sometime this year but no rush
