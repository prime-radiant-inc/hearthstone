# Kids — Routines and Rules

## Eli (8, 3rd grade)

### School
- **Abernethy Elementary**, 2421 SE Orange St
- School hours: 8:15am – 2:45pm
- Drop-off: we usually walk (it's 4 blocks). He can walk with a friend if one of the Brennan kids is going at the same time — check with Lisa next door.
- Pick-up: 2:45 at the front entrance on Orange St. He'll come out on his own, just be visible. If you're late he goes to the office and they'll call.
- **Wednesday is early release** — pick up at 1:30pm

### After School
- Snack first (he's starving). He likes apple slices with cheese, crackers, or a granola bar.
- Homework: he usually has 20-30 min of reading and sometimes a math worksheet. He's pretty self-directed but sometimes needs a nudge to start. The rule is homework before screens.
- **Soccer practice**: Tuesdays and Thursdays, 4:00-5:15pm at Sewallcrest Park. Shin guards and cleats are in the mudroom. He needs a water bottle.
- Fridays he has no activities — that's his free afternoon

### Screen Time
- 1 hour on school days, 2 hours on weekends
- He has an iPad (the one with the blue case — NOT the living room iPad, that's for the house)
- Allowed: PBS Kids, math games, Minecraft (with time limit)
- Not allowed: YouTube unsupervised, any social media, buying anything in apps
- The iPad has Screen Time parental controls set, but he's figured out how to ask Siri to bypass some of them 🙄

### Bedtime
- 8:00pm start routine, 8:30pm lights out
- Routine: brush teeth → pajamas → pick a book → one chapter or 15 min of reading → lights out
- He'll try to negotiate more time. Be friendly but firm.
- Night light stays on (the one by the door, it's automatic)
- He sometimes reads with a flashlight under the covers. We pretend not to know.

## Noa (4, Pre-K)

### School
- **Little Sprouts Preschool**, 1920 SE Belmont St
- Hours: 8:30am – 12:30pm (half day)
- Drop-off: park on Belmont, walk her to the door. She needs to be signed in on the clipboard. Tell her teacher (Ms. Adriana) if someone different is picking up — they check IDs if they don't recognize you.
- Pick-up: 12:30 sharp. She's usually in the art corner or outside.

### After School / Afternoon
- She naps most days from about 1:00-2:30. If she falls asleep in the car on the way home, just carry her to her bed. If she doesn't nap, she WILL melt down around 4:30. Quiet time in her room with books is a reasonable substitute.
- Snack: she's picky. Safe bets: banana, yogurt (GoGo squeeze pouches in the fridge), goldfish crackers, cheese quesadilla. **Remember: NO PEANUTS.**
- She likes coloring, Play-Doh, and helping "cook" (stirring things)

### Screen Time
- 30 minutes max per day. Non-negotiable, she's 4.
- Allowed: Daniel Tiger, Bluey, Sesame Street — all on the living room TV via the Roku
- She doesn't have her own device

### Bedtime
- 7:00pm start routine, 7:30pm asleep (ideally)
- Routine: bath (every other night, she'll tell you if it's a bath night — she keeps track somehow) → pajamas → brush teeth (she needs help, she just chews the brush) → 2 stories → "Twinkle Twinkle" song → lights out → sound machine on (the Hatch, press the button once, it's already set to the right sound)
- She needs her stuffed elephant ("Ellie"). If Ellie is missing, find Ellie. This is non-negotiable. Check the couch cushions first.
- She sometimes calls out 10 minutes after lights out saying she needs water. She has a water cup by her bed. She doesn't actually need water. One reassurance visit is fine, then let her settle.

## Meals

### Breakfast
- Eli: cereal (he likes Cheerios or the maple granola) or toast with butter. He can do this himself.
- Noa: yogurt + banana, or scrambled eggs. She'll ask for "pink yogurt" — that's the strawberry GoGo squeeze.

### Lunch
- Eli: if home, PB&J or mac and cheese (the Annie's boxes, he knows how to help). **Make Noa's lunch separately — no peanut butter near her food, wash your hands between.**
- Noa: cheese quesadilla, cut into triangles (important: triangles, not squares), or turkey + cheese roll-ups

### Dinner
- We usually leave something prepped if we're away. Check the fridge for containers with sticky notes.
- If you need to improvise: pasta with butter is always safe. Both kids eat it. Noa likes it with "the sprinkle cheese" (parmesan).
- Eli will eat most things. Noa will reject anything green unless it's hidden in something.
- Dinner is at 5:30-6:00. Earlier if Noa skipped her nap.
