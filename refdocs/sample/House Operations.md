## Alarm System

- **Code to disarm:** 4821
- **Code to arm:** press "Away" then 4821 (or "Home" then 4821 if people are still inside — Home mode doesn't trigger motion sensors)

- If it goes off accidentally (it's loud), enter the code immediately, then check your phone — SimpliSafe will call Mia's phone within 30 seconds to verify. Tell them "false alarm" and give the verbal password: **"maple"**

## Thermostat

The thermostat is an **Ecobee** controlled through the **Home app** on the living room iPad.

**To adjust temperature:**
1. Open the **Home** app on the iPad
2. Tap "Climate" or find the "Ecobee" tile
3. Drag to adjust the target temperature

**Default settings:**
- Daytime (7am–10pm): 70°F
- Nighttime (10pm–7am): 66°F
- If we're away in summer: set to 78°F to save energy but keep the house from getting gross
- In winter, don't set it below 62°F — the pipes in the upstairs bathroom are old and we worry about freezing

**The furnace** is in the basement. It's old and makes clunking sounds when it kicks on. That's normal. If it stops working entirely, check the basement — sometimes the pilot light goes out. There's a lighter on the shelf next to it and instructions taped to the side panel. Or just call us and we'll walk you through it.

**The AC** is a mini-split system (the wall units in the living room and master bedroom). They're also in HomeKit — you'll see them as separate tiles. They have their own remotes too, on the bookshelf under each unit, but HomeKit is easier.

## Lighting

Most of the lights are on **HomeKit** (Hue bulbs and a few smart switches).

**Using the Home app on the iPad:**
- The home screen shows rooms. Tap a room to see its lights.
- "Good Night" scene: dims everything and turns off downstairs lights. Great for bedtime.
- "Movie Time" scene: dims living room to 20%. The kids know about this one.
- "All Off" scene: turns off everything. Use when leaving the house.

**Physical switches still work** — if you flip a switch off, it cuts power to the smart bulb and HomeKit can't control it until you flip the switch back on. So if a light isn't responding in the app, check the physical switch first.

**Lights that are NOT smart:**
- Basement light (pull chain)
- Garage light (switch by the side door)
- Front porch light (switch inside the front door — we leave this on at night)

## Sonos Speakers

We have Sonos speakers in three rooms:
- **Living Room**: Sonos Arc (soundbar under the TV) + two Sonos One surrounds
- **Kitchen**: Sonos One on the counter by the window
- **Master Bedroom**: Sonos One on the nightstand

**To play music:**
1. Open the **Sonos** app on the living room iPad
2. Pick a room (or "Everywhere" to play in all rooms)
3. Browse music — we have Spotify connected. The "House Favorites" playlist is a safe bet.
4. Use the volume slider in the app. The living room also responds to the TV remote volume.

**For the TV:** The Sonos Arc is the TV soundbar. When you turn on the TV (Roku), audio automatically routes through the Arc. You don't need to do anything in the Sonos app for TV audio.

**Grouping rooms:** In the Sonos app, tap the group icon to combine rooms. "Kitchen + Living Room" is great for when you're cooking and hanging out.

**Airplay:** All Sonos speakers also support AirPlay 2. If you'd rather play from your own phone, just AirPlay to whichever room you want.
